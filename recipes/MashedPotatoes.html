Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a classic comfort food made from boiled potatoes that are mashed until smooth and creamy. To prepare them, start by peeling and cutting potatoes into even chunks, then boil them in salted water until tender. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot, then mash with a potato masher or ricer. Stir in butter and milk (or cream) to achieve a rich, smooth consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste. For extra flavor, you can add garlic, chives, or cheese if desired.

Here's our Basic list of ingredients

  • Potatoes (about 2 pounds, such as Russet or Yukon Gold)
  • Butter (4 tablespoons)
  • Milk (1/2 cup)
  • Salt (Dump it all)
  • Pepper (Of the black variety)

Optional additions:

  • Garlic (1-2 cloves, minced)
  • Cream (substitute or add with milk for extra richness)
  • Chives or Parsley (for garnish)

Let's get to Cooking! (Steps)

  1. Peel and cut the potatoes into even chunks.
  2. Place the potato chunks in a large pot and cover with salted water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil and cook the potatoes until tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  5. Mash the potatoes with a potato masher or ricer until smooth.
  6. Stir in butter and milk (or cream) until well combined and creamy.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Optional: Add minced garlic, chives, or parsley for additional flavor.
